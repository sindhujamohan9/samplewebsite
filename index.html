Biography

I am a postdoc in the Applied Mathematics and Plasma Physics group at Los Alamos National Laboratory. My mentors are Deepjyoti Deka, Harsha Nagarajan, and Russell Bent. Previously, I was a postdoc in the Wisconsin Institute for Discovery at UW-Madison where I was fortunate to work with Jim Luedtke. I obtained my B.Tech. in Chemical Engineering from IIT Madras, and my M.S. and Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering from MIT where I worked in the Barton lab.

My research interests lie at the interface of optimization and applied machine learning. I am particularly interested in exploring new paradigms that integrate machine learning and optimization under uncertainty, designing algorithms for their solution with theoretical guarantees, and developing scalable implementations for pressing energy systems applications.
