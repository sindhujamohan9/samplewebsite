Biography

I am a postdoc in the Applied Mathematics & Plasma Physics group at Los Alamos National Laboratory. My mentors are Deepjyoti Deka, Harsha Nagarajan, and Russell Bent. Previously, I was a postdoc in the Wisconsin Institute for Discovery at UW-Madison where I was fortunate to work with Jim Luedtke. I obtained my Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering from MIT where I worked in the Barton lab.

My research interests lie at the intersection of operations research, systems engineering, and applied machine learning. I am broadly interested in exploring new paradigms that integrate machine learning and optimization under uncertainty, designing algorithms for their solution with theoretical guarantees, and developing scalable implementations for pressing applications in energy and process systems.
